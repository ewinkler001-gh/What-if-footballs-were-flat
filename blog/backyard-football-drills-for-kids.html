5 Fun Backyard Football Drills for Kids (Easy Beginner Skills)

If you're looking for backyard football drills for kids, you're in the right place. Practicing at home is one of the best ways for young players to build confidence, improve skills, and have fun with the game.

These simple drills require minimal equipment and can be done in almost any backyard or open space.

1. Target Throw Drill

This drill helps kids improve accuracy and control when throwing the football.

  • Set up a target (bucket, cone, or chair)
  • Stand a short distance away
  • Have kids aim and throw at the target

Start close and gradually increase distance as accuracy improves.

2. Catch and Move Drill

This drill builds hand-eye coordination and movement skills.

  • Throw short passes to your child
  • Have them take a few steps before catching
  • Mix in different directions

This helps simulate real game situations.

3. Cone Weave Drill

This drill improves agility and footwork.

  • Set up cones or objects in a zig-zag pattern
  • Have kids run through them while holding the football
  • Encourage quick direction changes

This builds coordination and balance.

4. Snap and Throw Drill

This drill helps kids practice basic quarterback skills.

  • Start with a simple snap (hand-off or short toss)
  • Have the child throw the ball after receiving it
  • Focus on smooth motion

It’s a great way to build confidence for beginners.

5. Backyard Touchdown Game

Turn practice into a game by creating a small touchdown area.

  • Set up a goal line or end zone
  • Take turns running or passing the ball
  • Celebrate each touchdown

Making drills fun keeps kids engaged and excited.

Choosing the Right Football

Using the correct football size makes these drills easier and more enjoyable.

Building Skills Over Time

Consistency matters more than intensity. Short, regular practice sessions help kids improve faster than long, infrequent ones.

If your child is just getting started with the sport, you may also want to read our guide on flag football vs tackle football.

Keeping Football Fun

The most important goal is to make football enjoyable. When kids have fun, they are more likely to stick with the sport and continue improving.

Stories can also help build excitement. Books like What If Footballs Were Flat? combine humor and football to keep kids engaged both on and off the field.

Final Thoughts

Backyard football drills are a simple and effective way to help kids develop skills and confidence. With just a little time and creativity, you can turn your backyard into a fun training space.
