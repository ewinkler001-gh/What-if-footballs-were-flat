How to Get Kids Excited About Reading Using Sports

Many parents struggle to get their kids excited about reading. While some children naturally enjoy books, others would rather be outside playing sports than sitting down with a story.

The good news is that you can combine both interests. Sports-themed books are a powerful way to connect reading with something kids already love.

Why Sports Make Reading More Engaging

When children read about topics they care about, reading becomes more exciting and meaningful. Sports stories provide action, teamwork, and relatable characters that capture young readers’ attention.

For kids who love football, basketball, or soccer, reading about those sports feels less like homework and more like fun.

Start With Books About Their Favorite Sport

One of the easiest ways to spark interest in reading is to choose books about a sport your child already enjoys.

If your child loves football, football-themed stories can help them connect with reading in a natural way. Silly and imaginative stories are especially effective for younger readers.

For example, the playful children's book What If Footballs Were Flat? explores a funny "what if" scenario that encourages kids to think creatively while enjoying a football-themed adventure.

Make Reading Part of the Sports Routine

You can also build reading into your child’s normal sports routine. For example:

  • Read a short story before practice
  • Bring a sports book to games or tournaments
  • Use reading time as part of the bedtime routine

Linking books to sports activities helps kids see reading as a natural part of their daily lives.

Choose Books That Match Their Reading Level

The right reading level is important. Books that are too difficult can frustrate young readers, while books that are too easy may not keep their attention.

Picture books with humor and strong visuals are often ideal for younger readers, while older kids may enjoy chapter books about sports teams and athletes.

Use Sports Equipment as Reading Motivation

Another fun strategy is connecting reading goals with sports activities. For example, after finishing a book or chapter, kids might earn extra time practicing throws or playing catch.

You can even explore fun football-themed gift ideas that encourage both reading and play in our guide to Best Football Gifts for Kids.

Encourage Imagination Through Sports Stories

Stories allow kids to imagine new possibilities within the sports they love. Whether it’s a dramatic championship game or a silly twist like a football that suddenly goes flat, imaginative storytelling can turn reading into an adventure.

Encouraging creativity and curiosity helps children build a lifelong love of books.

Final Thoughts

Getting kids excited about reading doesn’t have to be difficult. By combining sports with storytelling, parents can help children discover that books can be just as exciting as the games they love to play.

When kids connect reading with their passions, they’re far more likely to build strong reading habits that last for years.
